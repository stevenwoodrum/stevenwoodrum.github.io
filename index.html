Hi! I'm Steven Woodrum,
an enterprising computer engineer
in my third year at the University of Virginia.

With a strong background in software development, circuit analysis, and burgeoning experience in electromechanical systems, I seek challenges that necessitate out-of-the-box thinking and multidisciplinary expertise.

I jokingly call myself a professional charge manipulator. Whether spinning a motor, filtering a signal, or shorting a battery, I'm ultimately controlling the flow of electrons to do something useful. Plus, it's fun to tell people my life revolves around particles I can't see.

Humanity's greatest challenges can't be solved with a 9-Volt and some duct tape. I've embraced this fact by adopting a high-level systems approach to problem solving. The UVa Technology Leaders Program has enabled me to develop design thinking practices in a multidisciplinary context not commonly seen in engineering degree curricula.
